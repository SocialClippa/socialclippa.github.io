Privacy Policy — TwitchClippa

Effective date: 21 June 2026

TwitchClippa ("the app", "we") is a desktop application that compiles video clips and, at the user's direction, uploads finished videos to the user's own social media accounts (such as YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram). This policy explains what data the app handles and how.

Who this applies to

TwitchClippa is a self-hosted desktop tool. It is operated by the individual user who installs and runs it on their own computer. There is no TwitchClippa server, hosted backend, or operator-run account system.

What data the app accesses

  • Authentication tokens. When you connect a social account, the platform's OAuth flow returns access and refresh tokens. These let the app upload videos to your own account on your instruction.
  • Basic account identity. After you connect an account, the app reads your account identifier and display name/handle so it can label the connected account in the interface.
  • Video files and metadata. The app processes video files and the titles, descriptions, and tags you enter for an upload.

How data is stored and used

  • All processing happens locally on your own device.
  • Authentication tokens are stored encrypted using your operating system's secure credential storage, and are used solely to perform uploads you request.
  • The app does not transmit your tokens, videos, or account data to the developer or to any third party other than the social platform you are publishing to (and only to perform the action you requested).
  • The app does not sell, rent, or share your data, and does not use it for advertising or profiling.
  • The app does not run analytics or telemetry that send your personal data off your device.

Third-party platforms

When you connect and publish to a platform (e.g. TikTok, YouTube, Instagram), your use of that platform is governed by that platform's own terms and privacy policy. The app interacts with those platforms' APIs only to carry out actions you initiate.

Data retention and deletion

Tokens and connected-account records remain on your device until you disconnect the account or uninstall the app. Disconnecting an account removes its stored tokens from your device. You may also revoke the app's access at any time from the relevant platform's account/security settings.

Children's privacy

The app is not directed to children and is intended for use by the account holder.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated; the effective date above will reflect the latest version.
